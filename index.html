About Me

Intentional Innovation & Equity

I am driven by the belief that technology must be used to tangibly improve the lives of Africans. Innovation should dismantle existing barriers, never reinforce them or exclude marginalised groups.


My technical foundation is anchored by a strong focus on data analysis using R and Python. More importantly, I synthesise these technical data insights to inform actionable national strategies. While my academic background specifically prepared me for the payments, economics, and financial sectors, my analytical interests span broadly across multiple developmental ecosystems.


Recently, my master's research has assessed the value of stablecoins within South Africa's modernising payment ecosystem. Through a comparative analysis of historical fiat pegs, stablecoin models, and domestic innovations like PayShap, my work argues that the primary, sustainable value of stablecoins lies in bridging critical cross-border remittance gaps. Ultimately, I propose a risk-based regulatory framework that mandates full, transparent collateralisation to safeguard financial stability while enabling global economic integration. Concurrently, as a Research Intern at Cenfri, I am deepening my exposure to developmental policy research, which is complemented by my global technical experience attending the 2025 Deep Dive into Blockchain summer school in Switzerland.
